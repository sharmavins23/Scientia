An ocean world, ocean planet, panthalassic planet, maritime world, water world, or aquaplanet, is a type of planet that contains a substantial amount of [[Water|water]] in the form of [[Ocean|oceans]], as part of its [[Hydrosphere|hydrosphere]]. The term is also commonly used for [[Astronomical Object|astronomical bodies]] with an ocean composed of a different fluid or [[Thalassogen|thalassogen]], such as [[Lava|lava]], [[Ammonia|ammonia]], or [[Hydrocarbon|hydrocarbons]]. The [[Earth]] is the only known astronomical object to presently have bodies of liquid water on its surface, though several [[Exoplanet|exoplanets]] have been found with the right conditions to support liquid water.
