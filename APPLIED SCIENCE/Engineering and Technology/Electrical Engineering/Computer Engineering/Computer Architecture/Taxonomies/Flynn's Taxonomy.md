Flynn's taxonomy is a classification of [[Computer Architecture|computer architectures]] proposed in 1966 and further extended in 1972.

Specifically, it covers four classifications:

1. Single instruction stream, single data stream ([[SISD]]);
2. Single instruction stream, multiple data streams ([[SIMD]]);
3. Multiple instruction streams, single data stream ([[MISD]]);
4. Multiple instruction streams, multiple data streams ([[MIMD]]).
